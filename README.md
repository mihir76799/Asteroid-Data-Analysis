# Asteroid-Data-Analysis

This project analyses the distance and relative velocity of asteroids drifiting through outer space with respect to earth by taking the csv data from NASA's records of asteroids from 1960 to 2060 (predicted). I have cleaned the data through Pandas and then used Matplotlib and Seaborn in Python to plot the graphs given below. Histograms of close-approach distance and relative velocity reveal that most asteroids pass Earth at distances between roughly 0.02 and 0.04 AU and typically travel at speeds between about 5 and 15 km/s, while a small number exhibit much higher velocities. The scatter plot compares the relative velocity of the asteroids with close approach distance. The distribution of points shows that asteroids with both high and low velocities can occur at various distances from Earth, suggesting no correlation between speed and distance in the dataset.

These visualizations help illustrate the distribution of near-Earth asteroid behavior and through this, researchers studying planetary motion and asteroid behaviour can understand the typical charactrisitics of asteroids, including their mean distance from earth and most probable velocities.
